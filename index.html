INFJ-A

INFJ-A is the rarest personality trait that makes up only 2% of the population and just 1% of men. Each of the four letters of the INFJ code signifies a key personality trait of this type. INFJs are energized by time alone (Introverted), focus on ideas and concepts rather than facts and details (Intuitive), and make decisions based on feelings and values (Feeling). My Project Idea

Overview

This project idea is going to address management issues with regards to scheduling maintenance activities and service or certification tasks. This idea, being in its infancy and currently only on paper, will take into account the possibility for scalability and flexibility with regards to new ideas that will improve and/or expand its scope or effectiveness. The project will be based on a Parent/Child design with a Web Portal/Parent interface used in conjunction with an App/Child on a phone or tablet. Simply put, the goal of this idea is to cut time lost to field administration.

Why is this even an idea?

Currently, maintenance schedules for field service technicians are bulky, unintuitive designs that are constantly looked down upon by technicians, almost all of which have great aversion to any form of administration activities that they are required to do. This quite often means that office administration has to spend time and effort to remind their field staff to complete these tasks. Another fault that this causes can be seen on my own current field.
I am a field technician in the elevator industry. Due to the niche nature of this industry being mainly occupied by qualified electricians, the wage enjoyed by the service men and women is one of the highest in the electrical field (barring FIFO occupations). This means that employers are relying on highly paid technicians to complete administrative activities usually completed by an office admin team or member. To further this issue, once the field team members complete and submit their paperwork concerning the relevant field tasks, the office admin team usually have to process these submissions further and send/email these completed formworks to their relevant destinations. Over time, the costs accrued by companies from these inefficiencies add up. Even with a crew of 4 technicians in the company I work for, the cost the company bears for us to tick and sign the same input fields on almost every maintenance task comes to almost $25,000 AUD a year! And there are multiple crews per state, times multiple states, times three countries. Keep in mind that this doesn’t take into consideration the further processing costs of the company’s service coordination/admin department.

What is going to fix this issue?

There are a lot of apps out there that aim to fill this space and some of them do an ok job (from personal experience and from reading reviews on what I have not experienced), but what none of them do, it seems, is do what WIX did for bloggers that did not fancy using WordPress. What this project aims to accomplish is to create a solution that can be easily employed in a simple, intuitive way that will cut administration costs generated by field team members. This can be done in a number of ways:

  • Pre-built templates available.
  • Saved technician details, signatures and maintenance tasks that can be filled at the touch of a screen when on-site.
  • Auto generated docs that are e-mailed to their relevant locations (eg. Job completion certificate e-mailed to the client).
The ways that this idea could also uniquely benefit potential users:
  • Available to freelance trades or medium sized businesses.
  • Video walkthroughs.
  • Easy set-up, easy use.
Initially, this project idea aims to start in a position where it can set its sights on achieving these goals in a methodical process in order for it to start in a feasible manner. Because there is available scope for an endless array of features eg. Contracts, invoicing, calendars, compatibility with other apps, HR management etc, it is important that this project first cover the simple and necessary basics and focus on streamlining these basics. It is possible that aiming for a huge scope while still on the starting line may provide such a drawback as to delay project in any sort of progress.
First, an online “main customer hub” or “portal” will be built to accommodate the first objective. This objective will be to:
  • Established a website.
  • Incorporated login features to access a basic profile or “portal”.
Secondly, customizable features will be added such as:
  • Build portal themes and user branding.
  • User profile.
  • User portfolio (units on service run, site contact details, maps integration etc).
  • Calendar and notifications.
Lastly, an app that can pull this information for the user and/or push completed tasks or added data (eg. site Facilities Manager changed his phone number, or key box code changed) to the main hub.
What is also going to be important to provide businesses and employers, is the opportunity to exercise control over their account. An example would be found in the employer locking the app so that employees can only submit certain data or use certain functions. This would mean that the employer has peace of mind that no one can accidentally change important information or details about a site/job that could cause un-intended negative consequences (eg. Employee deletes job, or mixes commencement dates etc). The ability for an employer to easily create a clean, intuitive, customizable work solution online (using a computer, laptop, netbook, phone etc) and have complete control over how that information is presented to his team via an app will be extremely attractive. Also, being able to cut unnecessary costs by having employees or solo workers in the field complete admin tasks at the touch of a button will certainly be notable and very welcome.
As mentioned before, if this idea is successful in its fulfilment and delivery, the scope of possibilities that can be enabled through proper groundwork will be further explored. In addition to simply filling out maintenance or repair schedules, the ability to add functions for quoting or invoicing can be added. An example of this feature would be a tradesperson quickly filling out materials, lengths, quantity, time etc into pre-built fields on the app while onsite and emailing the quote to the customer via a “submit” button to complete the task. Or some other options out of the vast variety would be:
  • Timing of technician’s attendance.
  • Location of devices so employers can prioritise labour for unplanned callouts.
  • Media uploads through the app.

How is this going to come to life?

Although good ideas are great, the skills and energy to turn ideas into something usable or enjoyable is even better. Good website and app development skills will be necessary to build this project along with some artistic skills to help present the completed design in a tasteful, inviting way. What will also be vital is the experience and feedback from a target market sample. This will ideally be a tradesperson who also understands small-to-medium business operations. This will greatly help in shaping the finished product into a tool that is made for its market, by its market’s population.

Conclusion

If successful, this will provide small-to-medium businesses the ability to vastly reduce the time spent on admin activities by that business’ field members. This in turn will reduce the costs associated while increasing the productivity of the user. In time, the ability for businesses to streamline All admin duties, not just for field teams, but also for HR departments, sales teams, accounts etc. of those businesses will also be implemented.
